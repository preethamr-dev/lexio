INCIDENT RESPONSE RUNBOOK
Version: 3.2 | Last Updated: 2024-01

INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION

P1 - Critical
Definition: Complete service outage or data loss affecting all customers.
Response SLA: Acknowledge within 5 minutes. Bridge open within 15 minutes.
Escalation: Page on-call engineer, on-call manager, VP Engineering simultaneously.
Customer communication: Status page update within 20 minutes.

P2 - High
Definition: Significant degradation affecting more than 25% of customers or a critical workflow.
Response SLA: Acknowledge within 15 minutes. Bridge open within 30 minutes.
Escalation: Page on-call engineer. Escalate to manager if unresolved after 30 minutes.
Customer communication: Status page update within 45 minutes.

P3 - Medium
Definition: Partial degradation affecting a minority of customers or a non-critical workflow.
Response SLA: Acknowledge within 1 hour.
Escalation: Assign to next available engineer. No overnight escalation required.

P4 - Low
Definition: Minor issue with minimal customer impact. Cosmetic bugs, logging gaps.
Response SLA: Triage within next business day.

DATABASE INCIDENT PROCEDURES

P1 Database Outage
1. Verify the outage is not a monitoring false-positive. SSH to at least two application nodes and confirm connection failures.
2. Check PostgreSQL primary health: pg_ctl status, check pg_stat_activity for long-running locks.
3. If primary is unresponsive, initiate failover to replica. Confirm replica lag was below 30 seconds before the incident.
4. Update DNS/connection strings to point at the new primary.
5. Verify application reconnection. Check application error rates drop within 2 minutes.
6. Root cause analysis required within 48 hours.

Escalation path for P1 database outage:
- On-call DBA (first contact)
- Infrastructure Lead
- VP of Engineering
- CTO if RTO exceeds 2 hours

NETWORK INCIDENT PROCEDURES

Packet loss or elevated latency:
1. Confirm with traceroute and mtr from multiple PoPs.
2. Check BGP session health on border routers.
3. If single-region issue, redirect traffic via global load balancer.
4. Contact upstream ISP if loss is on peering link.
5. Document all changes in the incident ticket.

COMMUNICATION TEMPLATES

P1 Initial customer notification:
"We are currently investigating an issue affecting [service]. Our team is actively working to resolve this. We will provide an update in 30 minutes."

P1 Resolution notification:
"The issue affecting [service] has been resolved as of [time]. All systems are operating normally. A full post-mortem will be published within 5 business days."

POST-INCIDENT REVIEW

Required for all P1 and P2 incidents.
Template location: /runbooks/post-incident-review-template.md
Owner: Incident commander assigns a primary author within 24 hours of resolution.
Deadline: Draft published within 5 business days.
Distribution: Engineering all-hands, affected customer success managers.
